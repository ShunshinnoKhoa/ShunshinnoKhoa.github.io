George Orwell 1984

Author

  • born on the 25th of June 1903 in Motihari as Eric Arthur Blair
  • dreamed of becoming famous writer since birth
  • famous for his allegorical novella Animal Farm and the dystopian novel 1984
  • died due to tuberculosis in 1947

Novel

  • published in 1949
  • 2005 choosen as one of the 100 best English-Language novels from 1923-2005
  • awarded a place on both lists of Modern Library 100 Best Novels
  • listed at number 8 on the survey The Big Read from BBC(2003)
  • There are three screen adaptations of the book(made in 1954, 1956 and 1984).

Plot

  • story is set in a dystopian world
  • Winston Smith, main character; lives in Airstrip One a province of Oceania
  • Oceania is one of three super states which are dominating the world.
  • Oceania is governed by the party which follows the ideology of incsog.
  • Winston Smith works at the Ministry of Truth.
  • He dreams of rebellion against the Party which makes him a thoughtcriminal.
  • beginns affair with Julia (against the rules because sex is only meant for reproduction) who shares his attitude towards the Party
  • He and Julia are both offered to join the Brotherhood by Mr. Charrington.
  • later is revealed to be an agent of the Party
  • Winston gets brainwashed to change his believes to fit the Ideology of the party.

Characteristics of a dystopian novel in the book

  • The society in the book is oppressed.
  • There are mesurements to control the society e.g surveillance.
  • There is one majour group in charge of everything.
  • It criticizes a political system(communism).
  • The Protagonist feels trapped and wants to break out of the System.

Current political problems criticised in the Book

  • censorship in books, films and other media (media manipulation, fake news)
  • increasing surveillance of people in certain states
  • Overall all problems occuring in a communism regime are being criticised.
  • George Orwell was inspired by the totalitarian regime under Stalin while he wrote the book.
  • What happens if someone breaks out of a dominating political or social reality.
