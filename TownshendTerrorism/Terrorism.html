Mathias - you raise a good question, which I address very briefly in the 3rd edition of the book. The IS was clearly not a pure terrorist group but it was not a conventional 'state' either: its deliberate breaches of international law were intended to terrorise. In its case no distinction between religious and ethnic or nationalist objectives could be drawn (just as in Islam there is no 'secular' sphere distinguishable from religion).
